Desktop Quick Start Guide

Follow this step-by-step workflow to create your first batch of ID cards

Download & Install Desktop App

Download IDLab for Windows from our download page. Install and launch the application to begin setting up your school profile.

💡 Tip: Make sure you have administrator privileges for installation.

Prepare Student Data (Tab 1)

Go to the "Pupil Data Entry" tab. Import your student list via CSV file or add students manually. Include names, exam numbers, grades, and other required information.

💡 Tip: CSV format: Name, ExamNumber, Grade, Class, etc. Use our template for best results.

Organize Student Photos (Tab 2)

Switch to "ID Photo Tools" tab. Upload student photos and use the Renaming Wizard to match photos with exam numbers automatically.

💡 Tip: Photos should be named with exam numbers (e.g., 2024001.jpg) for auto-matching.

Design Your ID Cards (Tab 3)

Navigate to "Generate IDs" tab. Select a template, customize with your school logo, colors, and branding. Preview how your cards will look.

💡 Tip: Upload high-resolution logos (PNG format) for best print quality.

Generate & Export Cards

Select students to include, choose export format (PDF or individual images), and generate your ID cards. Save the output for printing.

💡 Tip: Export as PDF for professional printing, or individual images for digital use.

Android Mobile App Tutorial

Capture professional student photos directly on your mobile device. Perfect for schools wanting to take photos on-site and sync with the desktop application.

Mobile Photo Capture

IDLab makes it simple to create pupil ID photos. You can import a list of pupils, take or attach ID photos, and save them directly to your phone.

To get started, you'll first need to export a pupil list from the IDLab desktop app after entering the pupil data.

Prerequisites - Prepare Your Data

Complete Desktop Setup First

Before using the mobile app, ensure you have your student data entered in the IDLab desktop application. This includes student names, exam numbers, and other details.

⚠️ Important: The mobile app works alongside the desktop app, not as a standalone solution.

Export Student List from Desktop

In IDLab desktop app, go to "Pupil Data Entry" tab and export your student list as a CSV file. Use a clear filename like "Grade12_2025.csv" and save it to an easily accessible location.

💡 Tip: Save to Desktop or Documents folder for easy access during transfer.

Install App & Transfer Data

Download IDLab Android app from our website. Transfer the CSV file to your phone using Bluetooth, USB cable, or cloud storage. Grant camera and storage permissions when prompted.

💡 Tip: USB transfer is fastest for large student lists.

Mobile Photo Capture Workflow

1. Import Student List

Launch IDLab Android app and tap the Upload icon (📤) in the top right corner. Browse and select your CSV file containing student names and exam numbers.

✅ Success: You'll see a confirmation message and student count after successful import.

2. Find Students Quickly

Use the search bar to find students by name or exam number. You can also filter by grade/class using the dropdown menu, or sort alphabetically for easy navigation.

💡 Tip: Search is instant - start typing and results appear immediately.

3. Capture Student Photos

Tap on a student's name to open the camera. Position the student properly, ensure good lighting, and tap the capture button. Review and confirm the photo or retake if needed.

🎯 Best Practice: Use consistent background and lighting for all photos.

4. Review & Manage Photos

Switch to the Gallery tab to view all captured photos. Photos are automatically named with exam numbers (e.g., 2024001.jpg). You can retake, delete, or export individual photos or entire batches.

📊 Progress: Check the progress indicator to see how many students still need photos.

5. Transfer Back to Desktop

Export photos from the Gallery and transfer them back to your computer. Place the photos in the same folder you use for the desktop app's photo import.

🔄 Workflow: Photos are now ready for the desktop app's "ID Photo Tools" tab.

Find Your Exported Photos

Once exported, you can find the photos on your phone at: Internal Storage → Pictures
